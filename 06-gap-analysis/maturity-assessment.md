# Maturity Assessment

## Purpose

This maturity assessment evaluates the current state of BayouTrust Digital Bank's cybersecurity and GRC control environment.

The purpose is to show how control areas can be rated by maturity level and compared against a target state.

## Maturity Scale

| Level | Maturity Rating | Description |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Initial | Processes are informal, inconsistent, or reactive. |
| 2 | Repeatable | Processes exist but are not fully standardized or documented. |
| 3 | Defined | Processes are documented and consistently performed. |
| 4 | Managed | Processes are measured, monitored, and reported. |
| 5 | Optimized | Processes are continuously improved and automated where possible. |

## Maturity Assessment Summary

| Domain | Current Maturity | Target Maturity | Gap | Summary |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Governance and Risk Management | 3 | 4 | 1 | Governance processes exist, but reporting metrics should be improved. |
| Identity and Access Management | 3 | 4 | 1 | IAM controls exist, but privileged access enforcement needs improvement. |
| Vulnerability Management | 2 | 4 | 2 | Scanning exists, but remediation tracking and escalation need improvement. |
| Logging and Monitoring | 2 | 4 | 2 | SIEM exists, but log coverage and alert review documentation are incomplete. |
| Incident Response | 2 | 3 | 1 | IR plan exists, but tabletop testing is not current. |
| Vendor Risk Management | 2 | 4 | 2 | Vendor reviews exist, but access reviews and monitoring need improvement. |
| Data Protection | 3 | 4 | 1 | Encryption exists, but data classification practices need standardization. |
| Backup and Recovery | 3 | 4 | 1 | Backups exist, but restoration testing evidence needs improvement. |
| PCI Readiness | 2 | 4 | 2 | PCI controls exist, but segmentation validation evidence is incomplete. |
| Audit Readiness | 2 | 4 | 2 | Evidence exists, but documentation quality is inconsistent. |
| Cloud Security | 2 | 4 | 2 | Cloud controls exist, but visibility and monitoring should be improved. |
| Configuration Management | 2 | 4 | 2 | Secure baseline evidence needs improvement. |

## Highest Maturity Areas

The strongest maturity areas are:

- Governance and risk management
- Identity and access management
- Data protection
- Backup and recovery

These areas have documented processes, but they still need stronger metrics, evidence, and monitoring.

## Lowest Maturity Areas

The lowest maturity areas are:

- Vulnerability management
- Logging and monitoring
- Vendor risk management
- PCI readiness
- Audit readiness
- Cloud security
- Configuration management

These areas require improved documentation, ownership, monitoring, and evidence collection.

## Target State

The target state is to move most major control domains to at least maturity level 4.

A maturity level 4 environment should include:

- Documented control procedures
- Assigned control owners
- Repeatable evidence collection
- Metrics and reporting
- Management review
- POA&M tracking
- Regular validation of control effectiveness

## Improvement Recommendations

Recommended improvement actions include:

1. Formalize control owner responsibilities.
2. Improve evidence request and review procedures.
3. Expand SIEM coverage.
4. Strengthen vulnerability remediation escalation.
5. Complete vendor access reviews.
6. Validate PCI segmentation.
7. Improve cloud asset visibility.
8. Conduct recurring control testing.
9. Create executive risk dashboards.
10. Track remediation through the POA&M.

## Summary

The maturity assessment shows that BayouTrust Digital Bank has a developing GRC program.

The organization has several controls in place, but maturity can be improved through better evidence, monitoring, ownership, metrics, and remediation tracking.
