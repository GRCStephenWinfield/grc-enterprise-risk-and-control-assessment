# Executive Summary

## Assessment Overview

BayouTrust Digital Bank completed a fictional enterprise risk and control assessment to evaluate its cybersecurity governance, risk management, and compliance posture.

The assessment reviewed key control areas including identity and access management, privileged access management, vulnerability management, logging and monitoring, incident response, vendor risk management, data protection, PCI readiness, SOC 2 readiness, and audit readiness.

## Overall Risk Rating

**Overall Risk Rating:** Moderate to High

The organization has several foundational security controls in place. However, improvements are needed in privileged access management, vulnerability remediation, vendor risk oversight, logging coverage, incident response testing, and PCI segmentation evidence.

## Top Findings

| Finding ID | Finding | Risk Rating |
|---|---|---|
| F-001 | Some privileged accounts are not protected by multi-factor authentication. | High |
| F-002 | Critical vulnerabilities are not always remediated within required timelines. | High |
| F-003 | Vendor access reviews are not consistently documented. | Medium |
| F-004 | Logging coverage does not include all critical systems. | High |
| F-005 | Incident response tabletop testing has not been completed in the last 12 months. | Medium |
| F-006 | PCI segmentation evidence is incomplete. | High |
| F-007 | Backup restoration testing is not consistently documented. | High |

## Business Impact

The identified gaps may increase the likelihood of unauthorized access, delayed threat detection, regulatory findings, service disruption, and audit issues.

The most significant business risks are related to customer trust, payment security, operational resilience, regulatory readiness, and the protection of sensitive financial data.

## Compliance Impact

The findings may impact the organization's ability to demonstrate alignment with the following frameworks and requirements:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- NIST SP 800-53
- CIS Controls
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
- PCI DSS
- Internal audit expectations
- Third-party risk management expectations

## Recommended Priorities

The organization should focus on the following remediation priorities:

1. Enforce multi-factor authentication for all privileged accounts.
2. Improve critical vulnerability remediation tracking.
3. Complete vendor access reviews.
4. Expand logging coverage for critical systems.
5. Conduct an incident response tabletop exercise.
6. Validate PCI segmentation evidence.
7. Improve backup restoration testing.
8. Formalize data classification procedures.

## 90-Day Action Plan

| Timeframe | Action |
|---|---|
| Days 1-30 | Enforce MFA for privileged accounts and identify the critical vulnerability backlog. |
| Days 31-60 | Complete vendor access reviews and expand logging for critical systems. |
| Days 61-90 | Conduct an incident response tabletop exercise and validate backup restoration testing. |

## Management Action Plan

Management should assign control owners, define remediation due dates, track progress through a POA&M, and provide monthly status updates to leadership.

The highest-risk findings should be remediated first, while medium-risk findings should be tracked through normal governance and compliance processes.

## Conclusion

BayouTrust Digital Bank has a developing cybersecurity and GRC program.

By addressing the top findings and executing the remediation roadmap, the organization can reduce enterprise risk, improve audit readiness, strengthen customer trust, and improve its overall control environment.
