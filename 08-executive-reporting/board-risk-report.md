# Board Risk Report

## Purpose

This Board Risk Report summarizes the cybersecurity and compliance risk posture of BayouTrust Digital Bank in a format intended for executive leadership and board-level oversight.

The purpose of this report is to communicate key risks, business impact, remediation priorities, and management actions without relying on overly technical language.

## Reporting Period

**Assessment Period:** July 2026  
**Organization:** BayouTrust Digital Bank  
**Report Type:** Cybersecurity and GRC Risk Summary  
**Overall Risk Rating:** Moderate to High

## Executive Risk Summary

BayouTrust Digital Bank has several foundational cybersecurity and GRC controls in place. However, the assessment identified gaps that may increase risk to customer data, payment systems, regulatory readiness, operational resilience, and executive oversight.

The highest-risk areas include privileged access management, vulnerability remediation, logging coverage, PCI segmentation evidence, and backup restoration testing.

Management should prioritize remediation of high-risk findings within the next 90 days and provide recurring status updates until the risks are reduced.

| Risk ID | Risk Area | Business Impact | Risk Rating | Management Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R-001 | Privileged Access Management | Unauthorized privileged access could lead to system compromise or sensitive data exposure. | High | Immediate |
| R-002 | Vulnerability Management | Delayed patching could increase exposure to cyberattacks and service disruption. | High | Immediate |
| R-003 | Logging and Monitoring | Limited monitoring coverage may delay detection of suspicious activity. | High | Near-Term |

| R-006 | Vendor Access Reviews | Incomplete vendor access reviews may allow unnecessary third-party access. | Medium | Near-Term |
| R-007 | Incident Response Testing | Lack of recent tabletop testing may reduce readiness during a security incident. | Medium | Near-Term |

## Key Board Questions

The board may ask management the following questions:

1. Are all privileged accounts protected by MFA?
2. How many critical vulnerabilities are overdue?
3. Are all critical systems sending logs to the SIEM?
4. Has the incident response plan been tested in the last 12 months?
5. Has backup restoration testing been completed for critical systems?
6. Has PCI segmentation been validated?
7. Are high-risk vendors reviewed on a regular basis?
8. Are remediation owners assigned and accountable?

## 90-Day Management Commitments

| Timeframe | Management Commitment |
|---|---|
| Days 1-30 | Enforce MFA for privileged accounts and identify the critical vulnerability backlog. |
| Days 31-60 | Expand SIEM logging coverage and complete vendor access reviews. |
| Days 61-90 | Complete incident response tabletop testing, backup restoration testing, and PCI segmentation validation. |

## Metrics Recommended for Board Reporting

Management should provide recurring reporting on the following metrics:

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Number of high-risk findings open | Shows unresolved risk exposure. |
| Number of overdue critical vulnerabilities | Shows patching and remediation performance. |
| MFA coverage for privileged accounts | Shows protection of high-impact accounts. |
| SIEM coverage for critical systems | Shows detection visibility. |
| Vendor access review completion rate | Shows third-party access governance. |
| Backup restoration test completion | Shows recovery readiness. |
| PCI segmentation validation status | Shows payment security readiness. |
| POA&M completion rate | Shows remediation progress. |

## Decisions Needed from Leadership

Leadership may need to make decisions regarding:

- Funding for SIEM expansion
- Staffing for vulnerability remediation
- Prioritization of privileged access improvements
- Vendor risk management resources
- PCI segmentation validation support
- Backup and recovery testing coordination
- Executive ownership of overdue risks

## Conclusion

BayouTrust Digital Bank has a developing cybersecurity and GRC program, but several high-risk gaps require management attention.

The board should expect recurring updates on privileged access remediation, vulnerability management, logging coverage, PCI segmentation, and backup recovery validation until these risks are reduced.
