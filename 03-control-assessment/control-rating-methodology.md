# Control Rating Methodology

## Purpose

This document defines the control rating methodology used during the BayouTrust Digital Bank GRC assessment.

The purpose of this methodology is to provide a consistent way to evaluate control design, operating effectiveness, evidence quality, and risk impact.

## Control Assessment Objectives

The control assessment is designed to determine whether controls are:

- Properly designed
- Operating as expected
- Supported by evidence
- Owned by the correct teams
- Aligned to cybersecurity frameworks
- Reducing risk to an acceptable level
- Ready for audit review

## Control Rating Categories

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| Effective | The control is properly designed, operating as expected, and supported by sufficient evidence. |
| Needs Improvement | The control exists, but there are design, operating, evidence, or documentation weaknesses. |
| Ineffective | The control is missing, poorly designed, not operating, or unsupported by evidence. |
| Not Tested | The control was not tested during this assessment period. |

## Design Effectiveness

Design effectiveness evaluates whether the control is properly structured to address the intended risk.

A control may be considered properly designed when:

- The control has a clear purpose.
- The control addresses the related risk.
- The control has an assigned owner.
- The control frequency is defined.
- The control activity is documented.
- The control aligns with policy or framework expectations.

## Operating Effectiveness

Operating effectiveness evaluates whether the control is actually working as designed.

A control may be considered operating effectively when:

- The control was performed during the assessment period.
- Evidence supports that the control operated.
- Exceptions were identified and addressed.
- The control owner followed the documented process.
- The control operated at the required frequency.

## Evidence Quality

Evidence should be reviewed to determine whether it is complete, accurate, current, and relevant.

| Evidence Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Sufficient | Evidence fully supports the control requirement. |
| Partially Sufficient | Evidence supports part of the control but has gaps. |
| Insufficient | Evidence does not support the control requirement. |
| Not Provided | Evidence was not received. |

## Risk Rating Considerations

Control findings are rated based on business, security, compliance, and operational impact.

| Risk Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Low | Limited impact if the control fails. |
| Medium | Moderate impact requiring management attention. |
| High | Significant impact to security, compliance, operations, or customer trust. |
| Critical | Severe impact requiring urgent leadership attention. |

## Control Testing Factors

The following factors should be considered when rating controls:

- Control design
- Control frequency
- Evidence quality
- Number of exceptions
- Business impact
- System criticality
- Data sensitivity
- Regulatory impact
- Repeat findings
- Compensating controls
- Management oversight

## Sample Rating Logic

| Scenario | Recommended Rating |
|---|---|
| Control is documented, operating, and evidence is complete. | Effective |
| Control exists, but evidence is incomplete. | Needs Improvement |
| Control exists, but exceptions are not tracked. | Needs Improvement |
| Control is missing or not performed. | Ineffective |
| Control could not be reviewed during the assessment. | Not Tested |

## Example Control Rating

| Control ID | Control Description | Design Effectiveness | Operating Effectiveness | Evidence Quality | Final Rating |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| IAM-001 | MFA is required for remote access users. | Effective | Effective | Sufficient | Effective |
| IAM-002 | Privileged accounts are reviewed quarterly. | Effective | Needs Improvement | Partially Sufficient | Needs Improvement |
| IR-002 | Incident response tabletop exercises are performed annually. | Needs Improvement | Ineffective | Not Provided | Ineffective |

## Documentation Requirements

Each control result should include:

- Control ID
- Control description
- Framework mapping
- Control owner
- Evidence reviewed
- Design effectiveness rating
- Operating effectiveness rating
- Final control rating
- Risk rating
- Finding description, if applicable
- Recommendation
- Remediation owner

## Summary

A consistent control rating methodology helps ensure that assessment results are fair, repeatable, and easy for management to understand.

This methodology supports control testing, gap analysis, risk register development, POA&M tracking, and executive reporting.
