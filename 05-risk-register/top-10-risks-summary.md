# Top 10 Risks Summary

## Purpose

This document summarizes the top enterprise cybersecurity and compliance risks identified during the GRC assessment.

The purpose of this document is to demonstrate risk identification, risk scoring, risk prioritization, and risk treatment planning.

## Risk Scoring Methodology

Risks are scored using likelihood and impact.

| Score | Rating |
|---|---|
| 1-5 | Low |
| 6-10 | Medium |
| 11-15 | High |
| 16-25 | Critical |

Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact

## Top 10 Risks

| Risk ID | Risk Statement | Likelihood | Impact | Rating | Treatment |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| R-001 | Privileged accounts may not be protected by multi-factor authentication. | 4 | 5 | High | Mitigate |
| R-002 | Critical vulnerabilities may not be remediated within the required service level agreement. | 4 | 4 | High | Mitigate |
| R-003 | Vendor access may not be reviewed on a regular basis. | 3 | 4 | Medium | Mitigate |
| R-004 | Logging may not provide full visibility into critical security events. | 4 | 4 | High | Mitigate |
| R-005 | Incident response procedures may not be tested annually. | 3 | 4 | Medium | Mitigate |
| R-006 | Asset inventory may not be complete or accurate. | 3 | 4 | Medium | Mitigate |
| R-007 | PCI segmentation evidence may be incomplete. | 3 | 5 | High | Mitigate |
| R-008 | User access reviews may not be fully documented. | 3 | 4 | Medium | Mitigate |
| R-009 | Backup restoration testing may not be performed regularly. | 3 | 5 | High | Mitigate |
| R-010 | Data classification practices may be inconsistent across business units. | 3 | 4 | Medium | Mitigate |

## Highest Priority Risks

The highest-priority risks are:

1. Privileged accounts without multi-factor authentication
2. Critical vulnerabilities exceeding remediation timelines
3. Incomplete PCI segmentation evidence
4. Incomplete logging coverage for critical systems
5. Inconsistent backup restoration testing

## Risk Treatment Approach

The organization should prioritize risk mitigation activities based on business impact, regulatory exposure, and likelihood of exploitation.

Recommended treatment actions include:

- Enforce multi-factor authentication for all privileged accounts
- Improve vulnerability remediation tracking
- Complete vendor access reviews
- Expand logging coverage for critical systems
- Conduct incident response testing
- Validate PCI segmentation evidence
- Improve backup restoration testing
- Formalize data classification practices

## Summary

The assessment identified several moderate and high risks that may impact confidentiality, integrity, availability, regulatory readiness, and customer trust.

The organization should address high-risk items first and track remediation through a formal POA&M.
