# Risk Acceptance Template

## Purpose

This template is used to document management acceptance of cybersecurity, compliance, operational, or third-party risk when remediation is delayed, not feasible, or not selected.

Risk acceptance should be formally reviewed, approved, time-bound, and supported by business justification.

## Risk Description

Describe the risk being accepted.

Example:

The organization is requesting temporary risk acceptance for a control gap where a legacy system does not support multi-factor authentication. The system remains in use for a business-critical process while a replacement solution is being evaluated.

## Business Justification

Explain why the risk is being accepted instead of immediately remediated.

Business justification may include:

- Technical limitations
- Business continuity requirements
- Vendor dependency
- Budget timing
- Project dependency
- Pending system replacement
- Operational constraints

## Impact Analysis

Describe the possible impact if the risk occurs.

Potential impact areas include:

- Customer impact
- Operational disruption
- Financial impact
- Regulatory impact
- Legal impact
- Security impact
- Reputational impact
- Audit impact

## Compensating Controls

List any controls that reduce the likelihood or impact of the accepted risk.

| Compensating Control | Description |
|---|---|
| Increased monitoring | Security team reviews related alerts more frequently. |
| Restricted access | Access is limited to approved users only. |
| Network restriction | System access is limited to approved network locations. |
| Manual review | System owner performs periodic review of activity. |

## Summary

Risk acceptance should not be used to ignore risk.

It should be used as a formal management decision when risk cannot be immediately remediated and compensating controls are in place.
