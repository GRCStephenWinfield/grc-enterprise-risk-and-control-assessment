# Assessment Methodology

## Purpose

This document describes the methodology used for the BayouTrust Digital Bank GRC assessment.

The purpose is to show how the assessment was planned, scoped, performed, documented, and reported.

## Methodology Overview

The assessment follows a structured GRC lifecycle:

1. Define scope.
2. Identify systems and stakeholders.
3. Select frameworks.
4. Map controls.
5. Request evidence.
6. Review evidence.
7. Assess control effectiveness.
8. Identify gaps and findings.
9. Score risks.
10. Develop POA&M and remediation plan.
11. Prepare executive reporting.
12. Support audit readiness.

## Step 1: Define Scope

The assessment begins by defining:

- In-scope systems
- Out-of-scope systems
- Business processes
- Data types
- Control domains
- Stakeholders
- Assessment limitations

Clear scope helps ensure that the assessment remains focused and repeatable.

## Step 2: Identify Frameworks

The assessment uses multiple frameworks and standards, including:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0
- NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5
- CIS Controls v8.1
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
- PCI DSS v4.0.1

The frameworks are used to support control mapping, evidence requests, risk assessment, gap analysis, and audit readiness.

## Step 3: Map Controls

Controls are mapped across frameworks to identify common control requirements.

Control mapping helps reduce duplication and supports a unified control approach.

Examples of mapped areas include:

- Access control
- Vulnerability management
- Logging and monitoring
- Incident response
- Vendor risk management
- Data protection
- Business continuity
- Audit readiness

## Step 4: Request Evidence

Evidence is requested from control owners to validate whether controls are designed and operating effectively.

Evidence may include:

- Policies
- Procedures
- Reports
- Screenshots
- System exports
- Tickets
- Review approvals
- SIEM dashboards
- Vulnerability scans
- Backup reports
- Vendor assessments

## Step 5: Review Evidence

Evidence is reviewed for:

- Relevance
- Completeness
- Timeliness
- Accuracy
- Approval
- Traceability to the control

Evidence gaps are documented and may result in control findings.

## Step 6: Assess Controls

Controls are assessed for:

- Design effectiveness
- Operating effectiveness
- Evidence quality
- Risk impact
- Compliance alignment
- Audit readiness

Control ratings include:

- Effective
- Needs Improvement
- Ineffective
- Not Tested

## Step 7: Document Findings

Findings are documented when:

- A control is missing
- A control is not operating effectively
- Evidence is incomplete
- Exceptions are not tracked
- Risk is not properly managed
- Audit readiness is weak

Each finding should include:

- Finding ID
- Description
- Affected control area
- Risk rating
- Business impact
- Framework mapping
- Recommendation
- Remediation owner

## Step 8: Score Risks

Risks are scored using likelihood and impact.

**Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact**

Risk ratings include:

- Low
- Medium
- High
- Critical

Risk scoring supports prioritization and executive reporting.

## Step 9: Develop POA&M

Findings and control gaps are tracked through a Plan of Action and Milestones.

The POA&M includes:

- Finding ID
- Weakness description
- Framework reference
- Risk rating
- Remediation action
- Owner
- Target date
- Status
- Validation method

## Step 10: Prepare Executive Reporting

Executive reporting summarizes:

- Overall risk rating
- Top findings
- Business impact
- Compliance impact
- Remediation priorities
- 90-day action plan
- Management decisions needed
- Board-level risk themes

## Step 11: Support Audit Readiness

Audit readiness activities include:

- Confirming control owners
- Validating evidence quality
- Reviewing open findings
- Tracking POA&M progress
- Preparing walkthrough questions
- Organizing evidence repositories

## Assessment Outputs

The assessment produces the following outputs:

- Company profile
- Assessment scope
- Framework mapping
- Control assessment results
- Evidence request tracker
- Risk register
- Gap analysis
- POA&M
- Remediation roadmap
- Executive summary
- Board risk report
- Audit readiness checklist

## Summary

This methodology demonstrates a practical and repeatable approach to performing a GRC assessment.

It connects business context, frameworks, controls, evidence, risk, remediation, and executive reporting into one structured process.
