# Vendor Risk Management Procedure

## Purpose

The purpose of this Vendor Risk Management Procedure is to define how BayouTrust Digital Bank identifies, assesses, monitors, and manages cybersecurity and compliance risks related to third-party vendors.

This procedure supports the protection of sensitive data, payment systems, customer information, business operations, and regulatory obligations.

## Scope

This procedure applies to vendors that:

- Access company systems
- Store, process, or transmit sensitive data
- Support critical business processes
- Provide cloud or technology services
- Support payment processing
- Provide security, infrastructure, or application services
- Have remote access to company environments

## Vendor Risk Objectives

The objectives of vendor risk management are to:

- Identify high-risk vendors before onboarding.
- Assess vendor cybersecurity controls.
- Confirm contractual security requirements.
- Track vendor access.
- Review vendors on a regular basis.
- Identify and remediate vendor-related risks.
- Support audit and compliance readiness.

## Vendor Risk Tiers

| Tier | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| High | Vendor has access to sensitive data, critical systems, payment data, or production environments. | Cloud hosting provider, payment processor, managed service provider. |
| Medium | Vendor supports important business processes but has limited access to sensitive data or systems. | Business software provider, support vendor. |
| Low | Vendor has limited business impact and no access to sensitive data or critical systems. | Office supply vendor. |

## Vendor Onboarding Procedure

Before onboarding a vendor, the organization should complete the following steps:

1. Identify the vendor service or business need.
2. Determine what data, systems, or processes the vendor will access.
3. Assign a vendor risk tier.
4. Request vendor security documentation.
5. Review vendor security controls.
6. Confirm contractual security requirements.
7. Approve or reject vendor onboarding.
8. Document vendor risk decision.
9. Track vendor access and ownership.

## Vendor Security Documentation

Security documentation may include:

- Security questionnaire
- SOC 2 report
- ISO 27001 certificate
- Penetration test summary
- Vulnerability management summary
- Incident response summary
- Business continuity documentation
- Data protection documentation
- Insurance certificate
- Contract security clauses
- Subprocessor list

## Vendor Access Review

Vendor access should be reviewed on a regular basis.

The review should confirm:

- Vendor users are still active and authorized.
- Vendor access is still needed.
- Access levels are appropriate.
- Privileged vendor access is justified.
- Terminated vendor users have been removed.
- Vendor accounts are protected by MFA where required.

## Ongoing Vendor Monitoring

High-risk vendors should be reviewed at least annually.

Ongoing monitoring may include:

- Reviewing updated SOC 2 reports
- Reviewing updated security questionnaires
- Reviewing vendor incidents
- Reviewing contract changes
- Reviewing access lists
- Reviewing performance issues
- Reviewing changes to services or data access

## Vendor Risk Findings

Vendor-related findings should be documented and tracked.

Findings may include:

- Missing security documentation
- Weak access controls
- Lack of MFA
- Incomplete SOC 2 report coverage
- No incident notification process
- Missing data protection requirements
- Unclear subcontractor risk
- Incomplete business continuity evidence

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Business Owner | Identifies vendor need and confirms business use. |
| Vendor Risk Manager | Coordinates vendor risk assessments and ongoing monitoring. |
| Security Team | Reviews vendor security controls and technical risk. |
| Legal Team | Reviews contracts and security terms. |
| Procurement Team | Supports vendor onboarding and contract process. |
| GRC Team | Tracks vendor risk evidence, findings, and audit readiness. |
| System Owner | Reviews vendor access to assigned systems. |

## Vendor Offboarding

When a vendor relationship ends, the organization should:

- Remove vendor system access.
- Disable vendor accounts.
- Recover company data where appropriate.
- Confirm data deletion or return if required.
- Terminate integrations or access tokens.
- Document offboarding completion.
- Update vendor inventory.

## Compliance Mapping

This procedure supports requirements from:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- NIST SP 800-53
- CIS Controls
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
- PCI DSS

## Review Frequency

This procedure should be reviewed at least annually or when major changes occur to vendor management, regulatory requirements, or third-party risk expectations.

## Summary

Vendor risk management helps reduce risks created by third parties that access systems, data, business processes, or critical technology services.

A formal vendor risk procedure supports stronger governance, audit readiness, and protection of sensitive information.
