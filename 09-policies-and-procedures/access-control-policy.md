# Access Control Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Access Control Policy is to define requirements for granting, reviewing, modifying, and removing access to BayouTrust Digital Bank systems and data.

This policy supports the protection of sensitive data, financial information, customer records, payment systems, and internal business systems.

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Vendors
- Privileged users
- Cloud systems
- Internal applications
- Banking platforms
- Payment systems
- Systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive data

## Policy Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Ensure access is granted based on business need.
- Enforce least privilege.
- Require proper approval before access is granted.
- Protect privileged accounts.
- Review user access on a regular basis.
- Remove access when it is no longer needed.
- Support audit and compliance requirements.

## Access Control Requirements

### 1. Least Privilege

Users must only be granted the minimum access necessary to perform their job duties.

Access must be based on role, business need, and management approval.

### 2. User Access Approval

New access requests must be approved before access is granted.

Access requests should include:

- User name
- Business justification
- Requested system or application
- Requested role or permission level
- Manager approval
- System owner approval, when required

### 3. Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-factor authentication must be required for:

- Remote access
- Privileged accounts
- Administrative access
- Cloud management consoles
- Systems containing sensitive data
- Systems in the payment environment

### 4. Privileged Access

Privileged access must be restricted to authorized users only.

Privileged accounts must be:

- Approved before being granted
- Protected by multi-factor authentication
- Reviewed on a regular basis
- Monitored where technically feasible
- Removed when no longer needed

### 5. User Access Reviews

User access reviews must be performed on a regular basis for critical systems.

Access reviews should confirm that:

- Users still require access
- Access levels are appropriate
- Privileged access is still justified
- Terminated users have been removed
- Vendor access is still needed
- Approval evidence is documented

### 6. Termination and Transfer Access

Access must be removed or updated when a user:

- Leaves the organization
- Changes job roles
- Transfers departments
- No longer requires access
- Completes a vendor or contractor engagement

Terminated user access should be removed in a timely manner according to company procedures.

### 7. Vendor Access

Vendor access must be approved, limited, monitored where feasible, and reviewed on a regular basis.

Vendor access should be disabled when it is no longer needed.

### 8. Shared Accounts

Shared accounts should be avoided where possible.

When shared accounts are required for technical reasons, they must be approved, documented, monitored, and protected with compensating controls.

### 9. Access Logging

Access to critical systems should be logged where technically feasible.

Logs should be retained according to company retention requirements and reviewed based on risk.

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Users | Use access only for authorized business purposes. |
| Managers | Approve access requests and review access periodically. |
| System Owners | Confirm appropriate access levels for their systems. |
| IAM Team | Provision, modify, and remove access based on approved requests. |
| Security Team | Monitor access control risks and review privileged access concerns. |
| GRC Team | Review access control evidence and support audit readiness. |

## Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy must be documented, approved, and reviewed on a regular basis.

Exception requests should include:

- Reason for the exception
- Business justification
- Compensating controls
- Risk owner approval
- Expiration date

## Compliance Mapping

This policy supports requirements from:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- NIST SP 800-53
- CIS Controls
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
- PCI DSS

## Review Frequency

This policy should be reviewed at least annually or when significant changes occur to systems, business processes, regulatory requirements, or the control environment.

## Summary

Access control is a critical security function. Proper access governance helps reduce the risk of unauthorized access, fraud, data exposure, and audit findings.
