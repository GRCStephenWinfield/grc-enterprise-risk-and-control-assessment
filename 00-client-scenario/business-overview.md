# Business Overview

## Purpose

This document provides a business overview of BayouTrust Digital Bank, the fictional organization used for this GRC portfolio project.

The purpose is to establish business context so that cybersecurity risks, compliance obligations, control gaps, and remediation priorities can be connected to realistic business operations.

## Company Summary

BayouTrust Digital Bank is a fictional regional digital bank and fintech company that provides online banking, digital payments, customer account management, and business banking services.

The company supports consumers, small businesses, and commercial clients through web-based and mobile banking platforms.

## Business Services

BayouTrust Digital Bank provides the following services:

- Online banking
- Mobile banking
- Digital account opening
- Customer account management
- ACH payment processing
- Debit card services
- Business banking
- Digital payment services
- Customer support
- Third-party payment integrations

## Customer Base

The company serves approximately 500,000 customers across multiple states.

Customer groups include:

- Individual banking customers
- Small business customers
- Commercial banking clients
- Digital payment users
- Mobile banking users

## Business Units

| Business Unit | Description |
|---|---|
| Retail Banking | Supports individual customers with online and mobile banking services. |
| Digital Payments | Supports payment processing, ACH activity, and card-related services. |
| Business Banking | Supports small business and commercial banking customers. |
| Information Technology | Manages infrastructure, cloud platforms, applications, and user support. |
| Security Operations | Monitors security alerts, vulnerabilities, incidents, and technical risk. |
| Governance, Risk, and Compliance | Supports risk management, compliance, audit readiness, and control assessment activities. |
| Vendor Management | Manages third-party vendor onboarding, reviews, and access governance. |

## Key Business Processes

The assessment considers the following business processes:

- Customer authentication
- Online banking transactions
- Payment processing
- Vendor onboarding
- User access provisioning
- Privileged access management
- Vulnerability remediation
- Incident response
- Backup and recovery
- Audit evidence collection
- Executive risk reporting

## Sensitive Business Functions

The most sensitive business functions include:

- Customer account access
- Payment transaction processing
- Privileged system administration
- Cloud infrastructure management
- Customer data storage
- Vendor access to company systems
- Security monitoring
- Incident response
- Backup and disaster recovery

## Business Risk Drivers

BayouTrust Digital Bank faces several business risk drivers, including:

- Protection of customer financial data
- Regulatory and audit expectations
- Payment security requirements
- Third-party vendor risk
- Cloud security risk
- Unauthorized access risk
- Operational disruption risk
- Customer trust and reputation risk
- Cybersecurity incident risk

## Business Impact Considerations

Cybersecurity control failures could impact the business in several ways:

| Impact Area | Example Impact |
|---|---|
| Customer Trust | Customers may lose confidence if data or banking services are impacted. |
| Regulatory Readiness | Weak controls may result in audit findings or compliance issues. |
| Operations | Security incidents may disrupt banking or payment services. |
| Financial Impact | Incidents may result in response costs, fraud losses, or penalties. |
| Reputation | Public incidents may damage the company brand. |
| Legal and Contractual | Control failures may create legal, vendor, or contractual obligations. |

## Summary

This business overview provides the context needed to evaluate cybersecurity and GRC risk.

The assessment focuses on controls that protect customer data, payment systems, cloud infrastructure, vendor access, and critical business operations.
