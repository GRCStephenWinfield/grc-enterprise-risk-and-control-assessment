# Regulatory Drivers

## Purpose

This document summarizes the cybersecurity, compliance, audit, and regulatory drivers considered in the BayouTrust Digital Bank GRC assessment.

The purpose is to show how business operations, data sensitivity, payment activity, and technology risks influence the selection of frameworks and controls.

## Regulatory and Compliance Context

BayouTrust Digital Bank is a fictional digital banking and fintech organization.

Because the company supports banking services, payment activity, customer data, cloud platforms, and third-party vendors, the organization must maintain a strong cybersecurity and compliance program.

## Frameworks and Standards Considered

| Framework or Standard | Purpose in This Assessment |
|---|---|
| NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 | Provides a high-level structure for governing and managing cybersecurity risk. |
| NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 | Provides detailed security and privacy controls for control mapping and assessment. |
| CIS Controls v8.1 | Provides prioritized cybersecurity safeguards. |
| SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria | Supports readiness for service organization control reporting. |
| PCI DSS v4.0.1 | Supports payment card security readiness. |
| POA&M Methodology | Supports formal tracking of weaknesses, remediation actions, and milestones. |

## Business Drivers

The main business drivers for the assessment include:

- Protect customer financial information.
- Protect payment processing systems.
- Maintain customer trust.
- Support internal and external audit readiness.
- Reduce cybersecurity risk.
- Improve executive risk reporting.
- Strengthen vendor oversight.
- Improve incident response readiness.
- Improve business continuity and recovery capabilities.

## Data Protection Drivers

The company stores, processes, or transmits sensitive data, including:

- Customer personally identifiable information
- Financial account records
- Payment card data
- Authentication data
- Employee data
- Vendor data
- Security logs
- Audit evidence

Sensitive data increases the need for strong controls around:

- Access management
- Encryption
- Logging and monitoring
- Data classification
- Vendor management
- Incident response
- Backup and recovery

## Payment Security Drivers

Because BayouTrust Digital Bank supports payment-related services, payment security is a major driver.

Key payment security concerns include:

- Cardholder data protection
- Network segmentation
- Firewall controls
- Access restrictions
- Authentication
- Logging
- Vulnerability management
- Security policy documentation
- Audit evidence retention

## Third-Party Risk Drivers

The company depends on third-party vendors for cloud hosting, payment processing, technology services, customer support, security tools, and business operations.

Vendor risk drivers include:

- Vendor access to systems
- Vendor access to sensitive data
- Vendor security posture
- Vendor incident notification
- Contractual security requirements
- Ongoing vendor monitoring
- Vendor offboarding

## Audit Readiness Drivers

Audit readiness requires the organization to maintain:

- Documented policies
- Assigned control owners
- Evidence of control operation
- Risk register
- Control testing records
- Access review evidence
- Vulnerability remediation evidence
- Incident response testing evidence
- Vendor review evidence
- POA&M tracking

## Executive Oversight Drivers

Leadership needs visibility into:

- Top cybersecurity risks
- High-risk findings
- Remediation progress
- Audit readiness
- Regulatory exposure
- Vendor risk
- Operational resilience
- Resource needs
- Control maturity

## Summary

The regulatory and compliance drivers in this assessment are based on the needs of a fictional banking, fintech, and technology organization.

These drivers support the use of NIST CSF, NIST 800-53, CIS Controls, SOC 2, PCI DSS, risk management, POA&M tracking, and executive reporting.
